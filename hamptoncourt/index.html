Wedding

On this most special day, we shall exchange our vows within the grounds of Hampton Court Palace, framed by the splendour of its English gardens and the gentle reach of the River Thames. Thereafter, we shall raise our glasses in celebration, and carry the memory of it with us always.

Location

Hampton Court Palace sits on the Thames riverbank, known for its Tudor Architecture and Courtyards, Baroque Facades, as well as exquisite blooming gardens. Upon arrival, one feels to have stepped back in history, experiencing the life of royal palaces.

Post ceremony, we will reach the Mitre Hotel that was originally built in 1665 by King Charles II. In its boathouse, we are pleased to host our wedding reception to further cherish and celebrate the moment, alongside enjoying the beautiful scenery of the riverside.

Travel

Hampton Court is an easy train ride from central London; direct services from London Waterloo reach Hampton Court station in about 35 minutes, followed by a pleasant walk to the palace and The Mitre.
